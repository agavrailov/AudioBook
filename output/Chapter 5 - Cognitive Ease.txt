Welcome to Chapter The Characters of the Story of Thinking, Fast and Slow. In this chapter, Daniel Kahneman introduces two characters that represent the two systems of thought that he believes are at work in our minds. 

The first character is System 1, which is fast, instinctive, and emotional. It is the system that makes snap judgments and is responsible for our intuition. System 1 is constantly active and is the source of our automatic reactions and habits. 

The second character is System 2, which is slow, deliberative, and logical. It is the system that requires effort and is responsible for our conscious thought. System 2 is not always active and is the source of our conscious decisions. 

Kahneman believes that these two systems are in constant competition, and that System 1 often wins out over System 2. He also believes that System 1 is prone to biases and errors, and that System 2 is needed to help us make better decisions. 

Kahneman also introduces a third character, the Ego, which is the part of our mind that is responsible for our sense of self. The Ego is the part of our mind that is constantly trying to protect our self-image and maintain our sense of control. 

Kahneman argues that the Ego is the source of many of our biases and errors, as it is constantly trying to protect us from making mistakes. He believes that understanding the Ego is essential to understanding how our minds work and how we make decisions.
